# TAISI Website — Structure & Style Guide

## Site architecture

Two pages. The landing page establishes credibility. The programs page has details and the application.

### Pages

1. **`/` — Main page** (credibility / about)
2. **`/program` — Programs** (Summer Intensive + Fellowships + application)

---

## Page 1: Main page (`/`)

Someone hears our pitch in class, looks us up. This page answers: "Are these people legit?"

- **Hero:** Mission statement. "A student group at the University of Toronto focused on mitigating catastrophic risks from advanced AI."
- **Why we exist:** AI safety is urgent and talent-constrained. We run programs to help UofT students build skills and have impact.
- **What we run:** Two cards linking to /program — Summer Intensive and Intro Fellowships.
- **Our network:** Kairos-funded, peer network (Oxford, Harvard, MIT, Cornell), partnered with Trajectory Labs.
- **Contact:** hello@taisi.ca

Compact. Should fit on one laptop screen. No selling, no urgency, no apply button on this page.

## Page 2: Programs (`/program`)

Informational. Details about our programs with a path to apply.

### Summer Intensive section
- Program overview — what it is, format, schedule
- What you get — skills, portfolio, network, understanding of the field
- Logistics — dates, location, cost
- Apply button (present, not aggressive)

### Fellowships section
- Alignment + Governance tracks
- Currently closed, opens next spring

### FAQ
- Only questions not already answered by the content above

## Visual style

### Overall vibe

**Professional, clean, confident.** Think: "well-funded research program" not "student club."

References: Anthropic, MATS. Serious but not boring.

### Color palette

- Light mode. Clean white/off-white backgrounds, dark text.
- Accents: taisi-red, taisi-blue
- No bright gradients, neon, or hackathon energy

### Typography

- Headings: serif
- Body: sans-serif
- Monospace accents for labels and metadata

### Layout principles

- Generous whitespace but compact — don't spread thin content across many screens
- Mobile-first
- No clutter. Every element earns its place.

### Imagery

- Minimal. No stock photos.
- Abstract/geometric backgrounds OK
- Subtle gradient orbs for visual interest

---

## Navigation

Minimal top bar:
- **Left:** Toronto AI Safety Student Initiative + logo
- **Right:** Programs | **[Apply →]** (button)

---

## Copy tone

- **Direct.** Short sentences. No filler.
- **Confident, not salesy.** State what the program is. Don't oversell.
- **Informational.** The site confirms what they heard in person. It doesn't need to convince.
- **No jargon.** Plain language.
- **No urgency tactics.** No "apply before it's too late." The deadline is stated once as a fact, not as pressure.
