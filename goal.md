## TAISI Site Redesign — Design Brief

**Audience:** UofT undergrads, AI-curious, academically ambitious, not yet committed to AI safety.

**Primary goal:** Get them to apply (summer intensive now, fellowships later). Fallback CTA: mailing list.

**Core feeling in 5 seconds:** "This is a community of smart, driven students working on something that matters. People have done their fellowships and both learned a lot and had an awesome time. I want in."

---

### Key Design Decisions

1. **Real photos, not illustrations.** Every watercolor/AI-generated illustration gets replaced with real community photos (like the one you just shared — that's exactly right). This is the single biggest upgrade.
2. **Social proof front and center.** Numbers ("50+ fellows across 3 cohorts"), fellow quotes, and the network flex (MIT, Harvard, Cambridge, funded by Kairos — which is already in your copy and is great).
3. **One clear, seasonal CTA** in the hero. Right now: "Apply to Summer Intensive." When that closes: "Apply to Fellowship." When both are closed: "Join the mailing list." Always one button, always obvious.
4. **Trim copy ~30%.** The content is good but there's too much of it before you get to the structure. Lead with what matters, cut the preamble.
5. **Community section on homepage.** Grid of candid photos + short quotes from fellows. Not bios — vibes. "I came in knowing nothing about alignment. Now I'm doing interpretability research at Vector." This is how you show the community is cool without saying it.
